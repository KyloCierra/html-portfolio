About me

I've always been interested in learning how to program games, and I've also always feared that it would be too difficult. My earliest memories were of playing Excite Bike on a Taiawnese NES. I started learning programming at an early age (QBASIC) but I got horribly distracted and never properly learned.

Fast forward to my late thirties. Technology has developed far more and YouTube and Udemy exist too. Knowledge is really easy to come by. But I still haven't learned how to create programs. I always thought, "Man, I wished I had the dedication to just sit down and learn coding properly." So, with that, I decided I would take a web development course on Udemy and learn how to create web pages, and hopefully interactive pages with JavaScript.

I also love art and graphic design, and dreamed of an interactive comic experience with music that would play in the background too (this website exists now, WebToons).

There's so much out there to learn, and it's rather overwhelming. The world seems kind of crazy right now, with AI on the horizon as a huge unknown, the fear of a looming economic downturn and general unkindness to humans who aren't like us.

I definitely got sidetracked there. Maybe I have ADHD?
